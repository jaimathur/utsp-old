Interpretation Of  Class Separability Outputs

Class Separability Using Divergence:

******** Divergence between two classes ********

            class1      class2      class3     

class1      0.0000      125.0804    374.9428   

class2      125.0804    0.0000      305.8016   

class3      374.9428    305.8016    0.0000     

 

**** Average Divergence ****

D_avg_class1 =    250.0116

D_avg_class2 =    215.4410

D_avg_class3 =    340.3722

 

**** Transformed Divergence Index between two classes****

            class1      class2      class3     

class1      0.000000    99.999984   100.000000 

class2      99.999984   0.000000    100.000000 

class3      100.000000  100.000000  0.000000   

The above listing shows the three different types of divergence computed viz. divergence, average divergence and transformed divergence. You can choose a scale of your own on which to compare training samples of different classes. The spectral differences between two training samples are higher if the divergence values approach maximum value of specified scale. It signifies that they are good training samples.

Please note that transformed divergence values are always scaled between 0-100 irrespective of your choice of scale.

Class Separability Using JM Distance:

******** JM distance between two classes ********

            class1      class2      class3      class4     

class1      0.0000      2.0000      2.0000      2.0000     

class2      2.0000      0.0000      2.0000      2.0000     

class3      2.0000      2.0000      0.0000      2.0000     

class4      2.0000      2.0000      2.0000      0.0000     

The purpose and use of computing JM distance between two training samples is to quantify the spectral differences between them. Here the scale is pre-specified i.e. 0-2.

Closer the values of JM distance to the value of 2, better is the spectral difference between training samples and good for training classifiers.
