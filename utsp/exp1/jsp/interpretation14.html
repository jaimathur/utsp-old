Interpretation Of  Texture Analysis Outputs

 

The following set of images represents different textural features generated from sample image test1.img.

Angular Second Moment

Angular second moment (ASM) is a measure of homogeneity. If the difference between gray levels over an area is low then those areas are said to be having higher ASM values.

In figure above, purple colored regions (some of them are enclosed in blue bordered rectangle) are homogeneous and gray level differences are less frequent in those regions. The white colored regions represent heterogeneous areas having low values of ASM i.e. gray level differences are higher and more frequent in those areas.

Contrast

Contrast is the second texture feature. This texture feature highlights the homogeneous regions in an image. Here, the ‘white’ colored areas are homogeneous. It implies that the gray level differences are very low in those areas and the contrast values are lower for these areas.

Inverse Difference Moment 

Inverse difference moment (IDM) values are higher for homogeneous regions. Here all purple colored areas have high IDM values.

Entropy

All the white colored areas represent heterogeneous texture and entropy values are very high for the same areas. Other areas have low entropy values.
