Interpretation Of  Maximum Likelihood Classification Outputs

The image below is a composite image generated from Red, Green and Blue bands of sam1.jpg image.

You might have noticed few different features in the color composite image. For example, the patches in dark red represent forest, light red to pinkish patches represent vegetation (plants, shrubs etc), black patches represent water bodies, cyan area represent urban/built-up areas and darkish green patches along the larger water body (on left) represent muddy lands and the white shades on the same water body represent turbid water.

For demonstration purpose let’s say that there are six features/classes-forest, water bodies, urban/built-up, muddy lands, vegetation and turbid water. You will need to take samples of these different features. The sampling process may look like that is showed in the figure below.

Now, after taking sample training data (pixels representing features) from the image you will feed this to the MLC experiment. What you feed is the coordinates of these rectangular patches. Please refer to the related procedure provided within procedure tab in main menu.

The program will generate a classified image (color image in jpeg format). In addition to this you will also be provided with generic binary classified image for using it later in accuracy assessment. Anyway, after classification is successfully implemented on the above sample image we have the classified image, shown below. Please notice the different patches are distinguished by different colors.
