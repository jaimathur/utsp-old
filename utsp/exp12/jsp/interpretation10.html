The figure above is the original sample image (quickbird) provided in the virtual lab. The figure represents a landscape in RGB (Red-Green-Blue) model. Any RGB model will comprise of three primary colors and each represented on three independent image planes. When such composite image is put to display on any computer monitor, the component images combine together to form a composite color image as shown above. Here in this example, the RGB model is formed by using blue, green and red color bands.

Post conversion from RGB model to HSV (Hue- Saturation-Value) model

On implementing the experiment on steps laid down below, you will be able to generate through the program four images, viz., hue, saturation, value/intensity component images and a composite image of hue, saturation and value/intensity images.

[1] selecting conversion type i.e. RGB to HSV

[2] choose quickbird.jpg from scroll down menu

[3] choose the bands for red, green and blue components

Hue component

                                         

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Hue represents a color attribute that describes a pure color (yellow, red, purple). In other words, hue is itself which we perceive as color.

Saturation component

                                         

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Saturation represents degree of purity of a color. It may be considered to the amount of white mixed in with color.

Value/Intensity component

                                     

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

This component represents brightness or dullness of a color.
