Simulation of Control of Magnetic Levitation System
PD Control for desired pole placement
Aim

The aim of this experiment is to learn how to design the Proportional plus Derivative (PD) form of control for the Maglev system. We use a special form of PD controller, the derivative kick free configuration, that is widely used in industry. We design the PD controller to place the closed loop complex poles at desired locations in the complex plane. We validate the design through simulations. We also vary the parameters of the PD controller and examine their effect on the location of the closed loop poles.

Introduction

The Proportional plus Derivative form of control has two modes of action: the proportional (P) mode and the derivative (D) mode. While the P mode responds instantly and provides corrective action to the current error, the D mode provides anticipatory corrective action for future errors. The D mode also provides added stability to the feedback system, so that a higher P gain can be used for better response.


In this experiment, we learn how to design the PD parameters for the Maglev system so as to place the closed loop complex poles at desired locations. We saw that with P control only, we were unable to do this.


In the PD controller, we shall use the D mode in a form that is widely used in industry. In this form, the derivative action is applied to the measured output, instead of the error. This helps avoid the “derivative kick” that occurs with the ideal PD form for step changes in setpoint signal.
