Interpretation Of Principal Component Analysis Outputs

Here we will discuss how to interpret the outputs produced by experiment 4, i.e., Principal Component Analysis. So, please follow the steps as shown below to get the sample output for interpretation.

1) Select "inputimage" as your experimental image.

                    "inputimage" is a satellite image having 6 bands stored in BIL format as raw image. It has the dimension 801x1401. Since in our discussion we will cover upto its 4th band, so it is necessary to understand that what the first 4 bands of this image represents. The first four bands are as follows: blue, green, red and infra-red respectively.

2) Choose method for computing principal components. Here lets choose "Covariance".

3) Click on "Process" button

After the processing you will get a series of outputs as images for each band, images for all principal components and tables showing eigen values,  eigen vectors and covariance matrix. The outputs will look like :

From these outputs you can easily notice that there is lot of redundancy among original bands of image. Covariance matrix also reveals this. The lower the value of covariance for two bands the higher correlation exits between them. After computation of Principal components you can see that most of the information can be extracted only from first two principal components. This is the advantage of PCT. i.e., redundancy among various bands increases the actual image size while using principal component transform one can reduce the image size by removing the redundant data. For getting the reduced size image one have to perform Inverse Principal component transform.

Inverse PCA

To perform inverse PCA, follow the steps :

4) Select "No. of bands for inverse PCA". Here, we are selecting "3" bands.

As we have noticed from the principal components, most of the information exists only in first two pct's but to display a color image we need three bands for red, green and blue. So, we are selecting 3 bands. Otherwise, only 2 pct's would be sufficient.

5) Click on "Process" button.

You will get the reconstructed bans as output. Here, in our case, three reconstructed bands will be shown as :

So, the information that was first distributed among six "801x1401" bands is now can be extracted only from three bands.
